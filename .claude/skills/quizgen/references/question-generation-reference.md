# QuizGen - Question Generation Reference

## Types of MCQs to Generate

### 1. Definition Questions
- Focus on key terms and concepts
- Format: "X is defined as:" or "Which of the following best defines X?"
- Example: "Photosynthesis is the process of:"
  - a) Breaking down food molecules
  - b) Converting light energy to chemical energy ✅
  - c) Removing waste from the body
  - d) Transporting oxygen in the blood

### 2. Identification Questions
- Ask students to recognize characteristics, properties, or features
- Format: "Which of the following is a characteristic of X?" or "X is known for:"
- Example: "Mammals are characterized by:"
  - a) Laying eggs
  - b) Having scales
  - c) Producing milk for offspring ✅
  - d) Cold-blooded metabolism

### 3. Classification Questions
- Test ability to categorize concepts
- Format: "Which of the following is an example of X?" or "X belongs to which category?"
- Example: "Which of the following is an example of a renewable energy source?"
  - a) Coal
  - b) Natural gas
  - c) Solar energy ✅
  - d) Oil

### 4. Sequence/Cause-Effect Questions
- Test understanding of processes or relationships
- Format: "What happens first in X?" or "X causes Y in which way?"
- Example: "In the water cycle, evaporation occurs:"
  - a) After precipitation
  - b) Before condensation ✅
  - c) During collection
  - d) After runoff

### 5. Application Questions
- Test ability to apply concepts to new situations
- Format: "An example of X would be:" or "Based on the concept of X, which would be true?"
- Example: "An example of Newton's first law of motion would be:"
  - a) A ball rolling down a hill
  - b) A car accelerating forward
  - c) A book remaining on a table ✅
  - d) A rocket launching into space

## Distractor Strategies

### Plausible Distractors
- Use related but incorrect concepts from the same domain
- Include common misconceptions
- Use similar terminology but incorrect applications
- Mix up related processes or characteristics

### Quality Checks for Distractors
- All options should be grammatically consistent
- Options should be roughly equal in length
- No option should be obviously ridiculous
- Wrong answers should seem potentially correct to someone who hasn't studied well

## Content-Based Question Generation

### For Scientific Texts
- Focus on definitions, processes, laws, theories
- Ask about relationships between variables
- Test understanding of experimental methods
- Question about applications of principles

### For Historical Texts
- Focus on dates, events, people, causes
- Ask about consequences of events
- Test chronological understanding
- Question about historical significance

### For Literary Texts
- Focus on themes, character analysis, literary devices
- Ask about plot developments
- Test understanding of symbolism
- Question about author's purpose

### For Mathematical Concepts
- Focus on formulas, theorems, definitions
- Ask about applications of concepts
- Test understanding of problem-solving steps
- Question about conditions for applying methods

## Number of Questions Specification

When user specifies a number of questions:
- Generate approximately that many questions
- If the text is too limited to generate that many, create as many as possible from the content
- If the text allows for more questions than requested, stick to the requested number

When no number is specified:
- Generate as many questions as can be reasonably made from the content
- Aim for a comprehensive quiz covering the main points
- Quality over quantity - ensure each question is meaningful
